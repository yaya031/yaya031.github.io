    Anemia Exploratory Data Analysis

    Anemia is a complex medical condition characterized by a deficiency in healthy red blood cells, crucial for oxygen transport throughout the body. It can arise from various factors, resulting in different types of anemia, each with its unique causes and manifestations.

    About the Dataset:

    Anemia is a complex medical condition characterized by a deficiency in healthy red blood cells, crucial for oxygen transport throughout the body. It can arise from various factors, resulting in different types of anemia, each with its unique causes and manifestations.

    About the Dataset:

    The dataset used for this project contains several features related to anemia, including gender, hemoglobin levels, mean corpuscular hemoglobin (MCH), mean corpuscular hemoglobin concentration (MCHC), mean corpuscular volume (MCV), and the presence or absence of anemia.

    Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):
    1. Importing Libraries: Necessary libraries such as numpy, pandas, matplotlib, seaborn were imported for data analysis and visualization.
    2. Loading Data: The dataset was loaded and inspected for its structure and missing values.
    3. Visualizing Data: Various visualizations were created to explore the data, including pie charts and count plots to understand the distribution of anemia in the dataset based on gender and other factors.
    4. Categorical Feature Analysis: Analyzed the relationship between gender and anemia occurrence through grouping and visualization.
    5. Continuous Feature Analysis: Explored the distribution and characteristics of continuous features like hemoglobin, MCH, MCHC, and MCV, including histograms and statistical summaries.
    Feature Engineering and Data Cleaning:
    1. Normalization: Continuous features were normalized using preprocessing techniques to ensure uniformity in data representation and aid in further analysis.

    This exploratory data analysis project provides valuable insights into the factors associated with anemia. By examining various features and their relationships, it offers a better understanding of the dataset and sets the stage for further analysis or predictive modeling. The normalization of continuous features ensures that the data is prepared for advanced modeling techniques. Overall, this project demonstrates proficiency in data analysis and visualization techniques, essential skills for any data science or healthcare analytics endeavor.
